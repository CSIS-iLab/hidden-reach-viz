Organizing China’s 300-plus shipyards into these four tiers helps to demystify China’s murky shipbuilding ecosystem.

At first glance, China’s industry appears to be dominated by privately owned Tier-4 shipyards. About 74 percent of all shipyards identified belong in Tier-4.

In reality, state-owned shipyards lead by a wide margin in output. Shipyards belonging to state-owned shipbuilding giant CSSC (Tiers 1 and 2) are especially influential. In year(s) X, they accounted for X percent of China’s total tonnage.

At the apex of China’s system are Tier-1 shipyards—those producing both commercial vessels and PLAN warships. While only 12 of China’s 307 shipyards (4 percent) fall in this tier, they generate X percent of the country’s total output by tonnage.

This largely because state-owned firms have the infrastructure and capacity to produce much larger vessels. On average, hulls built by state-owned firms are twice as large as those built at private yards.
